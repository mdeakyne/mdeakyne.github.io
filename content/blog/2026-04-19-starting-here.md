Every data project I've ever worked on had the same hidden problem: the analysis was fine, but nobody knew what to do with it.

The numbers were right. The methodology was sound. The deck was clean. And then it sat in someone's inbox, waiting.

I've spent a lot of time thinking about why that happens — and what to do about it. The short answer is that data doesn't persuade people. Stories do. Data is the evidence. Story is what makes people care enough to act on the evidence.

This site is where I work that out in public.

I'll write about the craft of data storytelling — what it looks like when it works, and what goes wrong when it doesn't. I'll write about the tools I use and the ones I've abandoned. I'll write about reading, thinking, and the practice of making things worth reading.

Some of it will be polished. Some of it will be half-formed. All of it will be honest.

The plan is to show my work.
